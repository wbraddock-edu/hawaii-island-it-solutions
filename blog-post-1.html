Best Internet Providers in Honolulu

// internet · Jun 15, 2025 · 5 min read

Choosing the right internet provider in Honolulu is more complicated than it should be. The island's geography, aging infrastructure in some neighborhoods, and the presence of a few dominant providers means your options vary significantly depending on where you live. Here's our honest breakdown based on years of helping Honolulu residents and businesses troubleshoot their connections.

Spectrum (Charter)

Spectrum is the most widely available provider across Oahu, serving most neighborhoods from Kakaako to Kaimuki. Their cable internet offers speeds up to 1 Gbps on their top tier. The main downside is that speeds during peak hours (evenings, weekends) can drop significantly in densely populated areas. Pricing is competitive at around $50–$80/month for standard plans, with the caveat that promotional rates expire after 12 months.

Hawaiian Telcom

Hawaiian Telcom is the local incumbent provider and offers fiber internet (Fios) in many parts of Honolulu. Fiber is significantly more reliable than cable — you get symmetric upload and download speeds, which is crucial for video calls and remote work. If fiber is available at your address, it's generally our top recommendation. Pricing starts around $65/month for 200 Mbps symmetric.

Cox Communications

Cox serves some areas of Oahu and offers competitive cable internet packages. Their Gigablast tier offers speeds up to 1 Gbps. We find Cox customer service to be generally responsive, which matters when you're dealing with outages.

Starlink (Satellite)

For residents in rural parts of Oahu or on neighbor islands with limited options, SpaceX's Starlink satellite internet has been transformative. Speeds of 100–200 Mbps with lower latency than traditional satellite. The hardware cost ($599 upfront) is significant, but monthly service is $120/month — competitive for areas where no other fast internet exists.

Our Recommendation

Check availability at your specific address before deciding. If Hawaiian Telcom fiber is available, choose it. If not, Spectrum is typically your best cable option. For businesses, we strongly recommend a secondary connection from a different provider as a failover — internet downtime in Honolulu is more common than people expect, especially during storm season.
