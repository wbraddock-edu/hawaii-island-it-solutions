Common Computer Problems in Tropical Climates

// tips · Apr 5, 2025 · 5 min read

After years of repairing computers in Honolulu, we've developed a very clear picture of the problems that are far more common in tropical climates than in the mainland US. Understanding these issues can help you prevent them — and know when to bring your device in before minor problems become expensive ones.

Overheating

This is far and away the most common problem we see. Hawaii's warm ambient temperatures reduce the temperature differential between your laptop and the air — meaning the fans work harder to achieve the same cooling effect. Add in blocked vents (using a laptop on a bed or couch), accumulated dust, and dried-out thermal paste, and overheating becomes almost inevitable. Symptoms include random shutdowns, dramatically slower performance (thermal throttling), and fans running at maximum speed constantly.

Fan and Bearing Failure

In Hawaii's humid salt air, laptop and desktop fans accumulate grime faster than in dry climates. This increases the load on fan bearings, causing them to wear out faster. A grinding or rattling sound from your computer is a warning sign — ignored long enough, a failed fan leads directly to overheating and potential CPU or GPU damage.

Battery Degradation

Lithium batteries degrade faster at higher temperatures. Leaving a laptop plugged in and hot in a warm room accelerates battery aging significantly. Hawaii's warm climate means batteries here often need replacement sooner than mainland equivalents. Keep your laptop in a cool, ventilated location and avoid keeping it at 100% charge all the time.

Hard Drive Corrosion

Older mechanical hard drives (HDDs) are particularly vulnerable to the corrosive salt air near the ocean. We frequently see HDD failures in Honolulu homes within a mile of the coast. If you haven't already, now is a good time to upgrade to an SSD — solid state drives have no moving parts, are significantly faster, and are far less susceptible to humidity and salt damage.

Port and Connector Oxidation

USB ports, headphone jacks, and charging connectors oxidize over time in humid, salty air. If you're experiencing intermittent connection issues with cables and peripherals, oxidized contacts may be the culprit. We can clean these professionally — it's a surprisingly inexpensive fix.
